    Dissertation

    Generation of T. cruzi strains knock-out for IP6K and evaluation of homologous recombination (HR) repair capacity.

    The Tritrypanosomatids; Trypanosoma cruzi, Trypanosoma brucei sp and Leishmania spp are one of the most important disease-causing agents. These parasites are responsible for Chagas disease, African sleeping sickness and Leishmaniasis respectively and constitute for thousands of deaths annually around the world. There tritryps employ Homologous Recombination to generate genetic variability which increases successfully the rates of infection through increased surface molecules diversity. HR is a complex system of pathways that repairs DNA lesions, primarily the Double Stranded Breaks (DSBs) introduced in the tritrpy’s DNA when these agents are attacked by the mammalian immune system.

    Recent studies have reported new key players, phosphoinositol pyrophosphates (PPIPs), that have an important role in eukaryotic HR repair pathways. These studies have demonstrated stalled HR activity in cells that lack the IP6K which is responsible for the phosphorylation of IP6 to IP7 and subsequently phosphorylation of IP7 to IP8 through PP-IP5K. Despite their discovery more than 20 years ago, little is known about the mechanisms of involvement of the PP-IPs in HR in most of the eukaryotic model organisms. T. cruzi, therefore, is an excellent study organism to help further understand the involvement of PP-IPs during HR in tritrypanosomatids.

    In this study, T. cruzi IP6K KO CL-Brener strains are used to assess the participation of PP-IPs in HR. The study employs use of CRISPR-Cas9 technology to generate IP6K KO strains which are challenged with Ionization Radiation to induce DSBs. Characterization analysis of IP6K KO, IP6K add-back and WT is done to establish the genotypes of these lineages. The add-back approach is explored as a control so as to demonstrate the specificity of the assay and eliminate any bias due to possible off-targets. The growth curves are then done to observe the proliferation pattern of all lineages after IR treatment.

    The recruitment kinetics for HR repair pathways are established and compared if the DNA damage responses are different. The DNA repair kinetics by HR are analyzed by immunofluorescence assay (IFA) and western blot using antibodies to identify the presence of phosphorylated histone H2A (γH2A), RPA-1, and the HR key component Rad51. Proteomic analysis of the T. cruzi linegaes generated using mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS) to understand the role of PPIPs in HR DNA repair pathways. A further scRNA-Seq analysis is done so as to establish the involvement of PPIP kinases in HR not previously predicted.
